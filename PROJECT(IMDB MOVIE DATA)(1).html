def replace_outliers(dataframe,feature):   
    Q1=dataframe[feature].quantile(0.25)
    Q3=dataframe[feature].quantile(0.75)
    IQR=Q3-Q1
    upper_bound=Q3+1.5*IQR  
    lower_bound=Q1-1.5*IQR
    
                                                                                # capping-replacing all higher values with upper bound (75% percentile)
    dataframe[feature]=np.where(dataframe[feature]>upper_bound,upper_bound,dataframe[feature])
                                                                                # flooring -replace all lower values with lower bounf (25% percentile)
    dataframe[feature]=np.where(dataframe[feature]<lower_bound,lower_bound,dataframe[feature])

CONCULSION This analysis of the IMDB Movie dataset provided valuable insights into the film industry between 2006 and 2016. Revenue trends: A small number of blockbuster films contribute the majority of total revenue. Genre patterns: Drama and Action are the most common genres, but Action and Adventure tend to generate higher revenue on average. Ratings: Higher IMDB ratings generally correlate with better revenues, though exceptions exist. Director influence: A handful of directors consistently produce high-grossing films. Trends over time: The number of films released per year remained relatively steady, but revenues fluctuated depending on blockbuster releases. These insights can help film studios, producers, and marketers make data-driven decisions about which genres to invest in, when to release movies, and how to allocate budgets.
